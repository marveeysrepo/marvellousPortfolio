Who I am

I’ve always been the kind of person who learns by doing. As a kid, I used to make my own little light bulbs — wiring LEDs to batteries just to see them glow. I’d fix wall sockets, change lamp holders. I didn’t think of it as “learning” back then; I was just curious to understand how things worked and how to make them better.

That same curiosity drives me today as a self-taught web developer. I’m drawn to building things that work — clean, responsive websites that connect logic with design. Every new project teaches me something: a better way to write code, a smarter layout, a simpler solution.

For me, learning has never been about perfection — it’s about consistent progress. I’m always exploring new ideas, breaking them down, and rebuilding them to understand them more deeply. Web development is my craft, but learning is my lifelong habit.
